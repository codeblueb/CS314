Scheme Links

CS 314 Principles of Programming Languages

  • To download Racket, the scheme implementation we will use: http://racket-lang.org/download/ . Choose the “distribution” Racket and the appropriate “platform”, download, and install.

    This downloads a bunch of stuff. To run Scheme, start the application DrRacket. DrRacket supports a number of related languages. To get the version of Scheme we are using in class: Click on the Language menu (at the top of your screen or window) and select Choose Language. In the window that pops up click on “Other Languages”, and then select R5RS (under the heading “Legacy Languages”).

  • The ilab computers do not have DrRacket but they do have a command-line oriented version of it, mzscheme.

  • The book Sketchy Lisp looks very useful. (The link is to the full pdf of the book, so it is free.)   Parts that may be relevant to our class are the following sections: Sections 1 through 3, except you can skip or skim 1.4.3, 2.1, 2.3.1, 2.5, 2.6, 2.8, 3.2, and 3.5.  You can skip or skim all of Section 4.

  • A simple, quick introduction to Scheme, covering only a small part of the language, is at http://cs.gettysburg.edu/~tneller/cs341/scheme-intro/ . Note that when it refers to “all lab computers” and “our Unix systems” it is referring to computers at Gettysburg College, not ours at Rutgers.

  • The official specification of the dialect of scheme that we will use. This is for reference and to see what a language specification looks like: http://schemers.org/Documents/Standards/R5RS/HTML/

  • While informative, the official standard leaves a bit to desired in terms of usability. I've found the language reference for CHICKEN scheme to be a bit more easy to use. CHICKEN is a code project to convert Scheme in to C and vice versa. Regardless, it uses R5RS, which we'll be using for the course.

  • Do not read “Teach Yourself Scheme in Fixnum Days” (available on the web), at least until 314 is over. It teaches Scheme but does not teach functional programming. Scheme is used in 314 primarily as a way to learn functional programming. I am afraid this book will put you in the wrong mindset for this course.
